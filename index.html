Case study 01 · 0 → 1 product

"What if every D2C ops manager had a PM as a copilot?"

India has 80,000+ D2C brands. Most run delivery ops on WhatsApp, spreadsheets, and gut feel. When something breaks — a courier delay, an SLA breach, a warehouse bottleneck — the ops manager finds out from an angry customer, not from their tools. I designed StackedAI to fix that.

The problem in three acts

Act 1 — ops managers are firefighters, not planners

They spend 4–5 hrs/day on reactive tasks: chasing couriers, manually escalating RTO orders, updating spreadsheets. There's no system surfacing "your SLA breach rate this week is 22% — here's why."

Act 2 — data exists but nobody reads it

Shipway, Shiprocket, Delhivery dashboards generate data. But ops managers aren't analysts. They need decisions, not dashboards. Nobody has bridged that gap yet.

Act 3 — AI tools exist but aren't ops-native

ChatGPT can answer questions but doesn't know your courier's TAT, your SKU return rates, or your warehouse shift schedule. Context is everything — and context is missing.

The product — StackedAI

An AI copilot that sits on top of your existing logistics stack, reads your ops data in real time, and tells you what to do next — before the customer complains.

  • Daily ops brief — top 3 actions for today
  • SLA breach predictor with 6hr early warning
  • RTO recovery — auto-flag, auto-reattempt
  • NLP query: "why did returns spike Tuesday?"

Integrations (day 1)

  • Shiprocket, Delhivery, Ecom Express APIs
  • Shopify / WooCommerce order data

Case study 02 · India data story

"India's gig economy is built on borrowed time — and the product opportunity nobody is building."

India has 15M+ gig workers in delivery and ride-hailing. The platforms need them desperately. But the platforms have also made their income structurally unstable — and that instability is a product problem, not just a social one.

The income instability loop

Algorithmic surge pricing (driver side)

Earnings are unpredictable hour-to-hour. A driver earns ₹800 on Monday, ₹220 on Tuesday. No way to forecast income or plan expenses.

Zero income protection

No sick pay, no ESI, no PF. An injury = zero income. Churn rate for drivers >18 months on platform is >40% — burnout is the top reason cited.

Debt trap via instant loan products

Platforms now offer "instant cash advances" against future earnings at 2–4% monthly interest. 1 in 3 active drivers in Tier-1 cities has taken one. Most rollover.

The product opportunity — income stability as a feature

The platform that solves driver income instability will win the supply war. Retention is cheaper than acquisition. Churn costs ₹3,000–5,000 per driver to replace.

Feature idea: SteadyPay

Guaranteed minimum weekly payout (e.g. ₹3,500) funded by a small platform reserve, smoothed across fluctuating daily earnings. Drivers opt in; excess earnings above a threshold replenish the reserve. Income smoothing, not charity.

Feature idea: Shield — micro insurance built in

₹5/day deducted automatically. Covers: accident hospitalization (₹50K), bike damage (₹15K), 7-day income replacement. Underwritten by a partner insurer, distributed via the app. No agent, no paperwork.

Case study 03 · Fix India — public systems redesign

"IRCTC books 1.4M tickets a day. It shouldn't feel like punishment."

IRCTC is the world's largest railway ticketing platform by volume — and one of the most frustrating digital experiences in India. 800 million Indians depend on it. A PM with the right mandate could transform it. Here's exactly what I'd do first, and why.

1.4M
tickets booked daily on IRCTC
58%
of users abandon during Tatkal booking
4.2★
Play Store rating — should be 4.7+

Top 3 problems worth fixing

Problem 1 — Tatkal is a lottery, not a feature

Tatkal opens at 10am. The site crashes. Users with faster internet and auto-fill scripts win. Regular users lose. This isn't a technical problem — it's a queue management and UX problem. Virtual queues exist. IRCTC doesn't use them.

Problem 2 — PNR status is spread across 6 screens

To check if your booking is confirmed: PNR page → train status page → coach position page. None link to each other. A single "my trip" view would reduce support calls by an estimated 30%.

Problem 3 — Refund status is a black box

Cancel a ticket → get a reference number → hear nothing for 7 days. No push notification, no timeline. Users call the helpline. The helpline has a 45-minute wait. This is a solved problem in every other consumer app.

Proposed solutions

Fix 1 — Virtual queue for Tatkal P0

Users join the queue at 9:50am. Queue opens at 10am, processes in order of join time. No crashes. No unfair advantage for scripts. Modeled after CoWIN's slot booking — which India proved works at scale for 1B+ users.

High impactMedium effort

Fix 2 — "My trip" unified view P0

One screen: booking confirmation + PNR status + coach position + live train location + platform number. One deep link shareable with family. Built on existing APIs — just stitched together properly. Engineering effort: 2 sprints max.

High impactLow effort

Fix 3 — Refund tracker with push notifications P1

Post-cancellation: push at T+0 (acknowledged), T+24hrs (processing), T+refund (credited with exact amount). Three notifications. No call needed. Modeled on how Zomato handles refunds — a pattern Indian users already understand.

High impactLow effort

Why these, why now

All three use existing dataNo new backend needed. Better UX on top of what exists.
Measurable in 30 daysHelpline volume, app rating, abandonment rate — all trackable immediately.
Political will existsIndian Railways has publicly committed to a digital overhaul by 2026. This is the roadmap.

Having worked on multi-country go-lives, UAT bug resolution, and driver app sequencing at Shipsy — I know what "simple UX" costs to build on complex backend systems. These three fixes are tractable. Every Indian recruiter reading this has felt these problems personally. That's the point.
